## Sheikh Chilli's Tomb

### Location
Thanesar, 2 km from Brahma Sarovar

### Period
17th century (Mughal era)

### Status
ASI Protected Monument

### Historical Background
- Tomb of Sufi saint Abd-ur-Rahim (known as Sheikh Chilli)
- Spiritual teacher of Dara Shikoh (Mughal prince)
- Built during Shah Jahan's reign
- Associated with Sufi traditions

### Architecture

#### Main Tomb
- Central dome (18 meters high)
- Octagonal structure
- Red sandstone construction
- Mughal architectural style

#### Minarets
- Four corner minarets
- 12 meters height each
- Decorative work

#### Decorative Elements
- Persian and Arabic inscriptions
- Quranic verses inscribed
- Geometric patterns
- Floral motifs
- Carved screens (jaalis)

#### Complex
- Mosque adjacent to tomb
- Garden area
- Water channels (now dry)
- Boundary wall

### Archaeological Importance
- Example of Mughal architecture
- Historical Sufi connection
- Cultural heritage site
- Tourist attraction

### Conservation
- ASI maintains the monument
- Restoration work ongoing
- Protected under Ancient Monuments Act

### Timings
Sunrise to Sunset

### Entry
Free

### Photography
Allowed

### How to Reach
Auto/taxi from Kurukshetra city
