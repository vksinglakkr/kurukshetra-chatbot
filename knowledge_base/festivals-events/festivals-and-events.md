# Festivals & Events of Kurukshetra

## Gita Jayanti

### Overview
Celebrates birth of Bhagavad Gita

### Time
November-December (Margashirsha Shukla Ekadashi)

### Duration
Week-long International Gita Mahotsav

### Events
- Classical music and dance performances
- Seminars on Gita teachings by scholars
- Cultural programs showcasing Indian traditions
- Scholars and spiritual leaders from across India
- Yoga and meditation sessions
- Light and sound shows at Jyotisar
- Exhibition on Gita and Mahabharata
- Book fairs and cultural exhibitions

### Venue
- Multiple locations across Kurukshetra
- Main venue: Brahma Sarovar complex

### Attendance
Lakhs of visitors annually

---

## Solar Eclipse Festival

### Overview
Largest religious gathering in Kurukshetra

### Location
Brahma Sarovar (main), other sarovars

### Time
During solar eclipse days (as per astronomical calendar)

### Significance
- Considered most auspicious for holy bath
- Belief: Bathing cleanses all sins
- Equals visiting all pilgrimage sites

### Attendance
Millions of pilgrims - one of largest religious gatherings in India

### Facilities
- Temporary camps and shelters
- Medical facilities and ambulances
- Security arrangements
- Crowd management systems
- Lost and found centers
- Clean drinking water
- Sanitation facilities

---

## Kurukshetra Mahotsav

### Overview
Annual cultural festival showcasing Haryana

### Time
Usually February-March

### Duration
3-5 days

### Attractions

#### Cultural Performances
- Folk dances: Saang, Ragini, Dhamal
- Traditional music: Haryanvi folk songs
- Theatre performances
- Classical dance recitals

#### Crafts & Arts
- Handicrafts exhibition
- Local artisans showcase
- Pottery demonstrations
- Textile displays

#### Food Festival
- Traditional Haryanvi cuisine
- Street food stalls
- Local delicacies
- Regional specialties

#### Other Activities
- Sports competitions
- Cultural competitions
- Photography exhibitions
- Children's activities

---

## Somavati Amavasya

### Overview
Monthly celebration when Amavasya falls on Monday

### Location
Sannihit Sarovar (primary), other water bodies

### Significance
- Considered highly auspicious
- Bathing believed to grant merit of all pilgrimages

### Activities
- Holy dip in sarovar
- Temple worship
- Charitable giving
- Pind Daan (ancestor rituals)

### Attendance
Thousands gather

### Frequency
Occurs few times per year

---

## Maha Shivratri

### Overview
Major Shiva festival

### Venue
Sthaneshwar Mahadev Temple and other Shiva temples

### Time
February-March (Phalguna Krishna Chaturdashi)

### Celebrations
- Night-long vigil (jagran)
- Abhishek (ritual bathing of Shiva linga)
- Devotional singing and bhajans
- Fasting by devotees
- Special prayers and aarti

### Attendance
Large gathering of Shiva devotees

---

## Navratri

### Overview
Nine-day festival celebrating Goddess Durga

### Frequency
Twice yearly (Chaitra and Sharad Navratri)

### Main Venue
Bhadrakali Temple

### Celebrations
- Daily special pujas
- Durga Saptashati recitation
- Cultural programs
- Dandiya and Garba nights
- Prasad distribution
- Kanya Puja on Ashtami/Navami

### Culminates In
Dussehra celebrations

---

## Kartik Purnima

### Overview
Full moon in Kartik month (October-November)

### Significance
Auspicious for bathing in sacred waters

### Location
All major sarovars

### Activities
- Holy bath at dawn
- Deep daan (lighting oil lamps)
- Temple visits
- Charitable activities

---

## Visitor Information

### Best Time to Visit
- **Gita Jayanti (December)** - for cultural experience
- **Solar Eclipse days** - for grand gathering
- **October to March** - pleasant weather
- **Avoid peak summer** (May-June)

### Facilities During Festivals
- Free/subsidized accommodation in dharamshalas
- Medical camps
- Lost and found services
- Tourist information booths
- Parking arrangements
- Food stalls and langar (community kitchen)

### Booking Information
- Accommodation should be booked in advance
- Government guest houses available
- Private hotels and lodges

- District Administration
