# Sacred Water Bodies of Kurukshetra

## Brahma Sarovar

### Overview
Largest sacred tank in Kurukshetra

### Dimensions
- **Length:** 3,600 feet
- **Width:** 1,500 feet
- **Depth:** 15-20 feet
- **Total area:** 125 acres

### Historical Background
- Ancient mentions in Vamana Purana
- Current structure: Mughal period (16th-17th century)
- Renovated during Prithvi Raj Chauhan (12th century)
- British-era renovations (19th century)
- Mentioned in Ain-i-Akbari by Abul Fazl (1590 CE)

### Religious Significance
- Sacred for ritual bathing
- Believed to grant salvation
- Associated with creation mythology

### Annual Events
- Solar eclipse bathing (largest gathering)
- Gita Jayanti celebrations
- Somavati Amavasya pilgrimage
- Millions of devotees annually

### Archaeological Features
- Ancient stepped structures (ghats)
- Medieval water management systems
- Stone inscriptions
- ASI protected monument

## Sannihit Sarovar

### Overview
Major sacred tank 3 km from city center

### Religious Belief
- All 360 tirthas meet here on Amavasya
- Bathing equals visiting all sacred places
- Mentioned in Mahabharata and Puranas

### Features
- Approximately 500m circumference
- Natural spring-fed water
- Ancient banyan trees surrounding

### Pilgrimage
- Somavati Amavasya attracts massive crowds
- Considered especially auspicious
- Dharamshala for pilgrims

---

## Jyotisar Sarovar

### Overview
Small sacred pond at Jyotisar

### Features
- Site of Bhagavad Gita discourse (traditional belief)
- Circular shape
- Ancient banyan tree on bank
- Temple complex adjacent
- Managed by Haryana Tourism

## Ban Ganga

- Associated with Pandavas
- Created by Bhima's arrow strike (legend)
- Near Dayalpur village
- Ancient water source
