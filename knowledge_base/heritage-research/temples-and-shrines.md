# Temples and Shrines of Kurukshetra

## Sthaneshwar Mahadev Temple

### Overview
Ancient Shiva temple, source of "Thanesar" name

### Historical Importance
- Capital of Thaneshwar kingdom (6th-7th century CE)
- Pushyabhuti dynasty ruled from here
- Mentioned by Hiuen Tsang (Chinese traveler, 643 CE)
- Destroyed and rebuilt multiple times

### Current Structure
- Modern temple (rebuilt 20th century)
- Main deity: Lord Shiva as Sthaneshwar
- Near Brahma Sarovar

### Archaeological
- Ancient ruins underneath
- Medieval stone sculptures
- Brahmi and Devanagari inscriptions

### Festivals
- Maha Shivratri (major celebration)
- Shravan month prayers
- Daily rituals

## Bhadrakali Temple

### Overview
Dedicated to Goddess Bhadrakali (fierce Durga form)

### Location
Kurukshetra city center, near Brahma Sarovar

### Legend
- Associated with Mahabharata period
- Pandavas worshipped before battle (traditional belief)
- Historically a Shakti peeth

### Temple
- Medieval architecture
- Red sandstone construction
- Multiple renovations

### Worship
- Daily rituals
- Navratri celebrations (9 days)
- Tantric traditions

### Devotees Believe
- Grants wishes
- Protection from evil
- Prosperity blessings

## Jyotisar Temple

### Overview
Site of Bhagavad Gita discourse

### Location
6 km from Kurukshetra city, Pehowa Road

### Features
- Modern temple complex (20th century)
- Krishna-Arjuna chariot sculpture
- Sacred banyan tree (claimed 5000 years, unverified)
- Sound and light show
- Museum of Gita teachings

### Management
- Haryana Tourism Development Corporation
- Kurukshetra Development Board
- Well-maintained grounds

### Events
- Gita Jayanti (December)
- International Gita Mahotsav
- Daily discourse sessions

### Visitor Info
- Open all days, no entry fee
- Parking available
- Wheelchair accessible

---

## Sri Krishna Museum
Inside Jyotisar complex
- Krishna's life through paintings
- Gita teachings illustrated
- Sculptures and artifacts
